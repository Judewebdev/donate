Every day I wake up to another missing friend, classmate, or family member.

I knew Abu Momoh Tahir. We were in the same faculty at Ambrose Alli University in Ekpoma. Abu was lively, full of dreams. In March 2024, kidnappers murdered Abu and held his older brother, a doctor, for ransom. His family is still traumatized.

I knew Scottzy. He studied in the Faculty of Physical Sciences at the same university where I studied. Scottzy was kind, brilliant, and had his whole life ahead of him. His kidnappers demanded ₦5 million naira before going silent. His family still doesn't know if he's alive.

And these are just two. The list goes on.

As a Christian in Nigeria, I know the target on my back grows bigger every day.

Christians in Nigeria face systematic persecution — killings, kidnappings, and attacks that have become so common they barely make headlines anymore. But for those of us living here, it's not statistics. It's my daily reality.

Documentation BBC Article

I wake up every day asking myself: Am I next?

I'm fighting for my life. I've invested most of my money into a business that's currently at risk because of my environment. I have helped young adults and teens with free skill empowerment, and I'm someone with visions — but my environment is becoming toxic.

I've done my research. Kenya offers me safety, stability, and opportunity. I can continue to work remotely and assist young adults both remotely and physically as I'm strengthened.

The money is not for luxury — it's strictly for relocation logistics.

I've saved $1,400 and am continuing to save, but time is running out.

Please save my life by donating to this cause. You're helping my vision stay alive, and you're helping many lives just by donating.

But before donating, please take a moment to meet with me.

I want you to hear my story in person and get to know the person you are going to be helping.

God bless you.
